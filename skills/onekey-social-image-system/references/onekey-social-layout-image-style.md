# OneKey Social Layout And Image Style

This reference is derived from `/Users/jiangjie/Documents/社媒出图系统/brand-assets/OneKey_社媒图AI规范/OneKey_社媒图案例`. For the fuller historical case analysis, read `onekey-social-visual-style-history.md`.

## Core Identity

- The primary palette is strict: white `#FFFFFF`, OneKey green `#3BD23D`, and black `#000000`.
- Use green as the strongest brand signal: full background, thick border, major panel, or key accent.
- Keep the composition clean, direct, product-led, and high-contrast. Avoid decorative clutter, gradients, bokeh, cyberpunk styling, or arbitrary illustration.
- Use real product UI, approved logos, official partner logos, or clean geometric/icon assets. Do not let image models redraw OneKey or partner marks.
- The broader visual direction is Modern Tech + FinTech editorial: modular, minimal, data-led, international, and professional.

## Layout Families

- Layout may change from image to image. Do not force every post into the same composition when the message, asset shape, or hierarchy calls for a different structure.
- Choose the layout family by the strongest message and reading path first; use the examples below as starting points, not fixed templates.
- Do not default every formal output to the green-border / white safe-area frame. That frame is one reusable template among many, best for compact framed announcements, co-brand cards, or posts that need a strong bordered card feel.
- Text-only or ranking posts: white or green background, huge title or ranked list, thin dividers, small brand lockup at bottom.
- dApp integration posts: green background, large title at top, white rounded panel containing 2 to 8 app logos in an orderly grid, CTA at bottom.
- Single partner/co-brand posts: green outer field with large white card, centered partner lockup pair, generous empty space.
- Perps/market posts: very large ticker or pair name, simple subtitle such as `Supported Perps Market` or `Featured Asset`, one dominant market logo/badge, minimal CTA.
- Education/security posts: green background with white open-book/card shape, quote mark motif, large headline, short source tag such as `#OneKey 101` or `#OneKey Security Lab`.
- App/product posts: clean UI screenshot or device mockup with green brand field, large concise title, optional white information panel.
- Event/activity posts: dark or vivid background is allowed only when the subject requires it; keep OneKey green, black, or white as anchors and avoid excessive effects.
- Character/lifestyle posts: only use when brand campaign needs a lifestyle signal; image should be polished, product-adjacent, and anchored by simple OneKey text.

## Layout Selection From Copy

Before designing, classify the copy and choose a reference-case layout direction:

- Ranking/list copy (`Top Swaps`, ordered assets, leaderboards): use a mostly white list/table layout with strong title, row dividers, token icons, and small brand anchor. Do not use a framed card unless the list itself needs containment.
- Hiring, manifesto, campaign statement, or slogan: use a bold typographic poster. Large headline can sit inside a white card on green, or directly on white/green depending on contrast. Keep imagery minimal or absent.
- Feature announcement (`New Feature`, `Bluetooth Connection Support`): use modular blocks. A green feature band or green content block can be the hero; a white/green bordered frame is optional, not required.
- Major metric or milestone (`$10B`, volume, rewards, leverage): make the number the dominant visual element. Use full green, split green/white, or vertical oversized type when the metric is the hook.
- New chain/token/market support: use the asset or token mark as the supporting hero and the asset name as large type. Prefer clean white/green split layouts or a compact card only if it improves focus.
- Coming soon / pre-order / launch status: use large status words (`COMING`, `SOON`, `NEW`, `FEATURE`) and green bands as structural elements. Strong typographic composition can replace the bordered card.
- Product or hardware feature: use real product imagery or product UI as the visual anchor with short headline and source/brand cue. Full white, light gray, or green-backed layouts are all acceptable if product contrast works.
- Security tip or educational copy: use quote/guide-cover style, large readable headline, generous whitespace, and a small tag. Illustration or icon elements may support but should not crowd the headline.
- Co-branding or partnership: use centered lockups with generous whitespace, often inside a white card or minimal full-white layout. The green border template is acceptable when the card relationship is the message.
- Lifestyle/person/campaign visual: full green or image-led layout may be used. Brand text must stay simple and legible; avoid forcing a white card over the image unless needed for contrast.

If more than one family fits, choose the one that best emphasizes the single strongest message. The layout should feel like a selected OneKey case pattern, not a universal wrapper.

## Composition Rules

- Use a fixed 512 x 512 px canvas for OneKey social outputs. Do not use 2048 x 2048, dynamic canvas sizing, or brief-derived dimensions for standard social posts.
- Use a 448 x 448 px safe area. Text, logos, image elements, and main content blocks must stay inside the safe area.
- For layouts with an outer border or framed edge, use a 16 px border by default.
- Use a 16 px corner radius by default for rounded borders, framed edges, cards, panels, badges, and image containers. Keep this radius consistent across same-level elements in one composition.
- If the standard compact green-border template is selected, use a full 512 x 512 OneKey Green background with a white safe-area panel inset by 16 px (`x=16 y=16 w=480 h=480`). The white safe-area panel must keep a 16 px corner radius and no stroke. Do not make the white safe area square, and do not create the green border by adding a stroke to the white panel.
- Align major elements to clear axes: background blocks, inset cards, borders, logo/icon placement, image blocks, headline groups, CTA groups, and dividers should feel intentionally connected.
- Align logos and source assets by their visible artwork, not by invisible padding, SVG viewBox bounds, imported image frames, or component bounding boxes. If the visible logo begins inside its asset frame, offset the frame so the logo artwork itself lands on the grid or content axis.
- Use generous, consistent spacing rather than arbitrary offsets.
- Maintain a strong safe area around edges. Do not place important text or logos near the crop boundary.
- Use generous white space and clear grouping. Cards may be rounded, but avoid nested cards unless representing a repeated grid item.
- Prefer card-like modular information structure for social feed readability, especially product, integration, tutorial, asset, and market posts.
- Make one idea dominant. Secondary information should support scanning, not compete.
- For token/market posts, partner color may be used as a small accent, but OneKey green/black/white must remain visually dominant.
- Use repeated panels or grid cells only when the content is truly a list, integration grid, ranking, or comparison.
- Check C.R.A.P. before delivery:
  - Contrast: hierarchy and readability are clear across headline, logo, CTA, panels, and supporting text.
  - Repetition: typography, spacing, corners, dividers, badges, and icon/logo treatments repeat intentionally.
  - Alignment: elements share visible axes or grid relationships; no accidental drifting or uneven optical placement.
  - Proximity: related content forms clear groups, and unrelated groups have enough separation.

## Alignment And Equal Spacing Rules

Core principle:

```text
All elements must align to a clear visual axis. Equal spacing is used only within the same group or repeated elements. Spacing between groups must serve information hierarchy rather than mechanically average-filling the canvas. Social images need order, but not table-like sameness.
```

### Canvas And Safe Area

- Use a 448 x 448 safe area by default.
- The 448 x 448 safe area sits 32 px from each canvas edge.
- If an outer border or framed edge is used, default to 16 px unless the user specifies another value.
- If rounded corners are used, default the radius to 16 px unless the user specifies another value.
- For the standard green-border frame, the visible green border is the 16 px area around a rounded white safe-area panel. The white safe area remains rounded (`16 px`) and stroke-free. This is a template-specific rule, not a global requirement.
- Core content must stay inside the safe area: logo, headline, subtitle, core benefit, CTA, source image, and partner/market marks.

### Alignment Subject

- Align by visible artwork, not by layer bounds, SVG viewBox, transparent padding, imported image frame, or component bounding box.
- Logo alignment uses the visible icon, wordmark, or lockup edge.
- Partner logos, token icons, market marks, and screenshots align by their visible subject edge.
- Text alignment uses the actual text visual edge; left alignment is the default for information-heavy social posts.
- When a source asset includes transparent padding, offset the asset layer so the visible subject lands on the intended grid line or content axis.

### Primary Axes

Every composition must establish at least one clear axis:

- Left axis: preferred default for OneKey social images. Logo, headline, subtitle, core benefit, and CTA may share the same left edge.
- Center axis: use for co-brand lockups, single-logo hero visuals, and symmetrical layouts where hierarchy remains clear.
- Split axes: use for two-column layouts. Each column gets its own internal axis, and the two columns must relate to a shared grid.

Do not place elements by eye alone if the final result has no readable axis.

### Spacing Scale

Use a small spacing scale instead of arbitrary values:

- 4 px: optical correction or micro adjustment.
- 8 px: tight relationship inside a compact group.
- 16 px: standard same-group spacing.
- 24 px: headline-to-subtitle or label-to-content spacing.
- 32 px: module spacing.
- 48 px: large module separation.
- 64 px: major whitespace or breathing room.

Optical correction of 1-4 px is allowed when visible shapes require it, but it must not break the underlying axis or hierarchy.

### Equal Spacing

- Equal spacing applies to repeated items, same-level cards, same-row logos, same-column list items, and controls within one group.
- Same-level repeated elements must keep consistent width, height, radius, stroke weight, and gap unless content length clearly requires a controlled exception.
- Same-group vertical spacing should be equal or follow the spacing scale.
- Group-to-group spacing should usually be larger than same-group spacing.
- Do not distribute unrelated groups evenly just to fill the canvas. Mechanical equal spacing can flatten hierarchy and make the image feel like a table.

### Visual Grouping

- Related content stays close: headline + subtitle, metric value + unit, logo + brand name, source image + source label.
- Unrelated content needs stronger separation: brand anchor, headline group, core benefit group, source image, and CTA should not visually merge.
- If two elements appear equally close, they should be semantically related. If they are not related, increase spacing or change alignment.

### Figma Execution

- Use Auto Layout for same-group stacks, repeated items, cards, button-like labels, and logo grids whenever practical.
- Use fixed frames or hidden guides for the canvas, safe area, and major columns.
- Do not align imported SVG/image nodes by their outer frame when the visible artwork has internal whitespace.
- Create or use a visible-subject guide when a logo or image asset has transparent padding.
- Before delivery, verify the exported screenshot, not only the Figma coordinates.

### QA Checklist

Before finalizing:

- Did you inspect the final Figma screenshot or exported PNG, not only the layer coordinates?
- Are Roobert/MiSans used in the correct language and text-role weights?
- Is the OneKey logo the correct official App/common asset and background color variant?
- Are all colors limited to approved OneKey brand tokens, with no accidental off-brand greens or grays?
- Is all text aligned to a clear visual axis, with no drifting lines or accidental centering?
- Is same-group spacing equal or consistently following the spacing scale?
- Does group-to-group spacing support hierarchy rather than mechanically averaging the canvas?
- Are image elements sized, cropped, and weighted appropriately for their role and container?
- Is there a clear primary visual axis?
- Are logo and source assets aligned by visible artwork rather than layer bounds?
- Do headline, subtitle, core benefit, and CTA follow a clear reading order?
- Is same-group spacing consistent?
- Is group-to-group spacing larger or otherwise clearly hierarchical?
- Are repeated items equal in size, radius, and gap?
- Does any element look like it is drifting?
- Does the layout feel ordered without becoming mechanically table-like?
- Are all core elements inside the safe area?

## Image Style

- Product/UI images should be real, sharp, and inspectable. Avoid fake UI, unreadable screenshots, or AI-rendered text.
- Product screenshots are the preferred visual asset for app features, new versions, wallet UI, and product workflows.
- OneKey hardware/product renders must come from the Figma `OneKey 产品图` section (`nodeId: 17979:7763`) when available. Follow `onekey-product-image-usage.md` for product family choice, view choice, scaling, contrast, and styling restrictions.
- Token/Coin icons are preferred for new asset, market, perps, and chain-support posts, but must come from source assets.
- If the user prompt explicitly says `参考 OneKey 插画风格绘制 xx 元素`, it is allowed to create a supporting illustration element in the official OneKey illustration direction. Treat this as an asset-generation step only, not as final social image generation. The final composition must still use approved OneKey colors, fonts, logo rules, layout hierarchy, safe area, alignment, spacing, and C.R.A.P. checks.
- Generated OneKey-style illustration elements should follow the Figma `OneKey 品牌插画风格` section: minimal vector-like shapes, black/white/gray structure, controlled OneKey green accents, transparent background, no final text, no logos, no partner marks, no fake UI, no claims, no gradients, no glow, and no decorative styling added after placement.
- People imagery should be rare and used mainly for AMA, KOL, guest, or community-person content.
- 3D assets should feel realistically rendered, not cartoon-like, and should serve a product or activity theme.
- Logo/icon assets should be crisp, centered, and surrounded by enough padding.
- Do not crop, stretch, recolor, retype, or manually reconstruct OneKey logo assets.
- If a source image, partner mark, token icon, market logo, or screenshot does not fit the target container cleanly, re-extract or re-crop the asset from the original source before resizing the container. Do not stretch the image, crop away important marks/text, hide edges, or accept a visibly awkward fit just to preserve the current layout.
- When re-extracting assets from a supplied composite image, keep the source-based appearance intact and add enough transparent or visual padding so the asset can sit comfortably inside the Figma container.
- If a supplied image has a solid-color background and will be used as an in-image element, cut out/remove that solid background and export the asset as a transparent PNG before placing it. Do this for source partner marks, token icons, market logos, product cutouts, and other foreground objects unless the solid background is intentionally part of the final design.
- Do not place solid-background source images as raw rectangles inside a OneKey layout when the image is meant to read as a standalone object or logo cluster.
- Foreground images do not need additional styling by default. After extracting or sourcing a transparent PNG/SVG/image, place it cleanly as-is. Do not add decorative cards, circular containers, shadows, strokes, glows, orbit rings, badges, labels, filters, or other effects to partner logos, market logos, token icons, product screenshots, or cutout images unless the user explicitly requests that styling or the source brand guideline requires it.
- If an image needs better balance in the layout, solve it through scale, crop, alignment, spacing, or transparent padding first, not by adding decorative visual treatments.
- Photographic or lifestyle images should be used sparingly and should not overpower the brand lockup or message.
- Avoid random dot patterns, uncontrolled glow, generic 3D blobs, stock-style atmospheres, and non-brand color washes.
- If using screenshots, keep them clean and crop to the relevant UI. Do not clutter with many tiny interface elements unless the image is a market table/dashboard template.
